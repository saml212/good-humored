# Legal and Contractual Risk Checklist

For MECE section 5 (Legal and contractual risk) of Atlas findings.
This is not legal advice. It is a structured checklist for surfacing
items that require counsel review.

---

## IP ownership

- [ ] Who owns IP created by contractors or employees pre-founding?
      Unassigned IP is an H risk.
- [ ] Are all software dependencies (open-source or third-party)
      compatible with commercial use? GPL/AGPL in a commercial product
      without a commercial license is an H risk.
- [ ] Are patents registered? In which jurisdictions?
- [ ] Are there any active IP disputes or cease-and-desist letters?
- [ ] Is the company name / domain / trademark registered?

---

## Key contracts (customer and vendor)

**Customer contracts:**
- [ ] Are customer contracts assignable without customer consent on
      change of control? Non-assignability without consent = H risk
      (customer can walk at close).
- [ ] Are there MFN (most-favored-nation) pricing clauses?
- [ ] Are there exclusivity or non-compete provisions favoring customers?
- [ ] Are SLAs defined? What are the penalty/credit provisions?
- [ ] What are the termination-for-convenience terms?

**Vendor/supplier contracts:**
- [ ] Are critical vendor contracts assignable on change of control?
- [ ] Are there minimum purchase commitments?
- [ ] Are there single-source dependencies (one vendor with no backup)?

---

## Change-of-control provisions

Change-of-control clauses can cause contracts to terminate or
require consent at closing. Catalog every contract with one.

Priority order:
1. Any contract > 5% of revenue or > $500K annual value.
2. Debt/credit facility agreements (often require lender consent).
3. Material vendor agreements (SaaS platforms, data licenses).
4. Lease agreements (real estate and equipment).
5. Government contracts (often require government approval of assignment).

---

## Employment and key-person

- [ ] Are employment agreements in place for all key executives?
- [ ] Do key-person agreements include IP assignment, non-solicitation,
      and non-compete (check enforceability by state/country)?
- [ ] Are there golden-parachute or change-of-control bonus obligations?
- [ ] What are unvested equity amounts for each key person?
      (Retention risk at close.)
- [ ] Are there any pending HR disputes or EEOC complaints?

---

## Regulatory and compliance

- [ ] Is the company subject to sector-specific regulation?
      (Financial services: FinCEN/SEC; Health: HIPAA; EU: GDPR.)
- [ ] Are all required licenses current? Any pending renewals?
- [ ] Has the company received any regulatory notices or inquiries in
      the last 3 years?
- [ ] For GDPR/CCPA: is a DPA (Data Processing Agreement) required
      with customers? Is a DPA in place?

---

## Pending litigation

- [ ] List all pending, threatened, or recently settled litigation.
- [ ] Any claims > $100K are M risk until resolved.
- [ ] Any claims involving the core product IP are H risk.
- [ ] Check court records (PACER for US) even if seller self-reports
      none — sellers sometimes omit pre-litigation disputes.

---

## Rep-and-warranty risk surface

Standard reps in an M&A purchase agreement where DD findings feed
the negotiation:

| Rep area | DD source | Risk if silent |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| IP ownership | IP section above | H |
| No material litigation | Litigation section | H |
| Financial statements accurate | Financial quality section | H |
| No material adverse change | Management interviews | M |
| Contracts assignable | Contracts section | Varies |
| Compliance with laws | Regulatory section | M |

Where the data room is silent on a rep area, flag as an open question
and recommend rep-and-warranty insurance coverage for that rep.

---

## Common open questions for legal section

If the data room does not answer these, list them explicitly:

1. Copy of all contracts > $250K annual value with change-of-control
   language highlighted.
2. IP assignment agreements for all founders and early contractors.
3. Current cap table with all option grants and vesting schedules.
4. List of all pending or threatened litigation (last 5 years).
5. All regulatory licenses and permits with expiration dates.
6. Any government contracts (require separate assignment analysis).
